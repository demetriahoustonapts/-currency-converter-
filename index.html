Exchange Rate Guide (Not Thin Content)

Exchange rates show how much one currency is worth in another. Rates change due to inflation, interest rates, economic news, and global market activity. Your “real” rate is often different depending on where you exchange: banks, credit cards, cash kiosks, or transfer apps may apply markups and fees.

Common situations

  • Travel: Your credit card may offer a better rate than exchanging cash at a kiosk.
  • Online shopping: Some merchants apply “dynamic currency conversion” with poor rates—pay in local currency when possible.
  • Money transfers: Compare transfer fees + rate markups (both matter).
  • Business payments: Verify invoice currency and settlement currency to avoid surprises.

FAQ

Why is my bank’s rate different?
Banks and cards may add a markup, and many also include fees. Two providers can use the “same” market rate but produce different totals.

What rate should I enter?
Use the rate from your bank, transfer provider, or a reliable reference source, then compare the total including fees.

Is this tool exact?
No — it’s an estimate tool to help you understand conversion math. Always confirm final rates before buying or transferring.

Do you store my inputs?
No. Conversions happen in your browser. See the Privacy Policy for more details.
