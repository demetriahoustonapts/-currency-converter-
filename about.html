About QuickCurrency

QuickCurrency is a simple currency conversion tool and educational website built to help people understand exchange rates in plain language. If you’ve ever asked, “Why does my bank show a different rate than Google?” or “Why did I pay more than I expected at checkout?” — you’re exactly who this site is for.

Our mission is to provide easy conversion math, practical tips, and clear explanations so you can make smarter decisions when traveling, shopping online, sending money, or comparing international pricing.

What QuickCurrency Does

  • Quick estimates: Convert amounts between common currencies using a rate you enter.
  • Education: Explain how exchange rates, markups, and fees affect the final total.
  • Comparison help: Help you compare providers (banks, cards, transfer apps) with fewer surprises.
  • Simple language: No complicated charts required—just clear examples.

What QuickCurrency Does NOT Do

QuickCurrency is not a bank, money transfer service, or payment processor. We do not execute transactions, store your financial account information, or guarantee final conversion totals. Conversion results shown are estimates for informational purposes only.

Why Rates Differ (The “Real-World Rate”)

Many people assume there is “one” exchange rate. In reality, the rate you get depends on where and how you exchange. A provider can show you a rate that looks close to the market rate but still charge more by adding:

  • Rate markup: A hidden margin added to the mid-market rate.
  • Foreign transaction fees: Often applied to international card purchases.
  • Transfer fees: Fixed fees or percentage-based fees on money transfers.
  • ATM fees: Charges from both the ATM operator and your bank.
  • Dynamic currency conversion (DCC): “Pay in your currency” at checkout—often worse rates.

Common Use Cases

  • Travel planning: Estimate daily budgets and compare cash vs card spending.
  • Online purchases: Understand checkout conversion and avoid DCC when possible.
  • Sending money: Estimate how much the recipient receives after fees and rate markups.
  • Freelancers: Convert invoice amounts and compare payout options.
  • Businesses: Quick estimates for international pricing and vendor comparisons.

Transparency & Accuracy

We aim to provide clear information, but rates change constantly. Your final amount depends on your provider. Before making a purchase or transfer, always confirm the final rate and fees with your bank or platform.
